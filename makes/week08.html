Week 8 – Networks of Knowledge & Power

The Artifact

Process Notes

I used Kumu and various sources in order to construct a network detailing AI supply chains. When making, I wanted to identify important resources, countries who supplied these resources, and who used the resources and for what.

Reflection Prompt

Reflect 8: Who is visible/invisible in AI networks? How does visualization reveal or obscure power?

Reflection Response

In AI networks, the final product is revealed, but the means stay hidden. Take the supply chain of AI resources as an example. If we want to track something like silicon, we start where it is mostly produced. We then arrive at China, and from here we can track where this resource goes, what is made, and what final product is ultimately constructed.

But one thing we do not see is right at the beginning, the actual production of silicon. Those who had to mine it, the actual local people responsible for this, are hidden from the public eye. This lack of visualization obscures these people's power, as it allows for large companies to benefit from their labor and keep them working these exploitative jobs.
