The Artifact

Process Notes

I used Datawrapper to make a graph of the research that I collected. The sources that I used ranged from news articles to a source that came directly from google themselves (in the case of defending their service, Gemini). When it came to what data was the hardest to find, I would say that it definitely was data pertaining to specific C02 emissions figures cumulatively and annually for AI services. For example, when it came to the case of finding data for ChatGPT, I was only able to find unofficial figures and estimates for the total amount of C02 emissions that this service produced. This is not to say that these estimates are incorrect or inaccurate in any way, but I would have liked to have official statements and statistics from the makers of these AI goods. When it comes to what I was uncertain about, it actually comes in the form of the total pull of AI in terms of adding to the world's total C02 count. For example, the figure for the per query C02 emissions for ChaptGPT is only 4.32 grams. Of course, there are a multitude of people around the world making searches, which makes for many cases of this figure, all adding up to a larger amount of C02 emissions. But even then, if we compare it to something such as a commercial flight, which uses 157 grams per kilometer, the question really boils down to, what are we more willing to have contribute to the worlds C02 count? I think that the root of my uncertainty comes in the form of not knowing this answer. Of course, I am not a big proponent of AI usage, but could I really cite environmental concerns as the main issues when things that I use and do in my day-to-day life are contributing just as much to C02 emissions?

Reflection

AI in itself is not sustainable, but nothing on this earth truly is. When we look at sustainability on Earth, I feel it is most appropriate to look at the core value of economics, which is the allocation of limited resources to meet unlimited demand. The reason I feel as if this is important is because we live in a predominantly capitalist world, where forces and motivations nationwide and worldwide are driven by economic decisions. AI is the newest thing that is in, the newest trend that people are demanding, and therefore it is now see as more appropriate now then ever to allocate whatever necessary resources to meet said demand. This is inherently not sustainable, but we are tolerant to this fact due to our willingness to allocate more of our limited resources to this new and developing technology. Any water, electricity, or land being used is simply a byproduct of this tolerance.
