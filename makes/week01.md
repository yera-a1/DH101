## Process Notes
This week, I analyzed Photogrammar, an AI project dedicated to the mapping of photos across the United States from 1935 to 1944. 

## Reflection
When it comes to this project, I think the making lies most heavily in the supplied photos and UI for the website. To start, the photos are 100% human captured. The photographers that took the photos that are used on the website had to go out in the world and actually find the subject matter for their compositions. Even greater, they had to have an keep in mind to try and portray with their images a certain prompt they wanted to capture. For example, if they want to show to other people the effects of war in a given area, they will need to find things that perfectly encaptualte that feeling. Being able to do this in a variety of different ways is very human. Secondly, the UI for the website is a human element as well. To make the best interface possible, it has to be made by humans, tested by humans, all for humans. Sure AI could give a go at trying to design something inherarently human, but at the end of the day the final product was something that humans had to consciousnessly make. Overall, the photos and UI interface of this project are the things that count as “making” in this project.
