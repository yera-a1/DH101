Week 6 – Text & Distant Reading

The Artifact

Process Notes

I used Voyant and ChatGPT in the process of text analysis. With Voyant, I had to consider the data that was laid out in front of me, while with ChatGPT I was allowed to sit by as it summarized the text for me. I specifically analyzed Edgar Allan Poe's "The Raven" using both tools to compare how AI and traditional visualization methods approach literary analysis.

Reflection Prompt

Reflect 6: What do we gain/lose when machines "read" literature for us?

Reflection Response

When machines read literature for us, we gain efficiency but lose competency. AI, as we have discussed previously, lacks a certain human "soul" or emotions, and while it can identify emotions in text, it simply does not have the capacity to truly understand them. So what does that mean when it interacts with literature, a very soulful and human medium? It makes for reflection that is devoid of any speculation or understanding.

Say that you want an AI to summarize a writing piece, for the sake of efficiency and time. The AI then pulls out a bulleted point summary of the reading, taking the subjective parts of it and putting it on the screen. You would be content with this theoretically, but let's say you then decide to actually read the passage to see how accurate it is. When you do, you see lots of small things that the AI missed. Non-essential or large ideas, but ones nonetheless that add to the overall meaning of the passage and interpretation of the reading.

The AI had one task: to summarize. But it was not told to interact with the text, to think about it critically, or to think about what it means. There is no prompt you can tell the AI that would make it do this, as it simply can't. This is what is lost with AI. Critical interaction that allows for the finding of hidden and subtle meaning.

Attribution & AI Use

  • Tools used: ChatGPT, Voyant
  • AI prompts (summary): "Summarize the poem, 'The Raven', by Edgar Allan Poe" and "Can you give me an in depth analysis"
  • What AI generated: See above images
  • What you changed or decided: N/A

Key Insight

When AI reads literature for us, we gain efficiency but sacrifice the critical engagement that leads to discovering subtle meanings and nuances. AI summaries are useful for quick overviews but cannot replicate the deep, speculative understanding that comes from human reading and reflection.
