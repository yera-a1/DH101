Week 3 – Selfie & Identity

The Artifact

AI-generated selfie images created using DALL·E based on personal description.

Process Notes

Tools used: DALL·E

Uploaded an image and initial prompt for generation, then updated prompts to refine the output with specific modifications.

The Making Process

Initial prompt described personal characteristics: 18-year-old African American male, 5'8", 185 pounds, ~4-inch afro with low taper, straight teeth with slight chip on front tooth.

Follow-up modifications requested:

  • Slightly larger muscles
  • Cleaned up hair
  • Studio-quality lighting
  • Streetwear styling (baggy jeans)

Reflection

An "authentic" self cannot truly exist when co-constructing with AI. The pure, authentic self might exist in the raw image uploaded to AI models—representing us in our most physical form. However, the moment we use AI to manipulate this image, in any manner, we are no longer truly "authentic."

There exists an argument that AI can allow us to construct a form of ourselves that we see as true, but this raises deeper questions about what authenticity truly means. Assuming authenticity means how we are in the present moment, then AI cannot work alongside identity construction in a way that maintains traditional notions of authenticity.

Attribution & AI Use

  • Tools used: DALL·E
  • AI prompts: Personal description + refinement requests
  • What AI generated: Two final images with modifications
  • What you changed/decided: Direction of modifications and final selection
