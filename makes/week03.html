Week 3 – Selfie & Identity

The Artifact

DALL·E was used to generate and modify both of these images. I uploaded an image and a prompt for the initial image generation, then an updated prompt to generate a second image.

Process Notes

I used DALL·E to generate AI-based selfie images based on my personal description. I uploaded an initial image and prompt, then refined it with updated prompts to modify various aspects of the output.

Reflection Prompt

Reflect 3: What does "authenticity" mean when identity is co-constructed with AI?

Reflection Response

An "authentic" self cannot truly exist when co-constructing with AI. The pure, authentic self might exist in the raw image uploaded to AI models—representing us in our most physical form. However, the moment we use AI to manipulate this image, in any manner, we are no longer truly "authentic."

There exists an argument that AI can allow us to construct a form of ourselves that we see as true, but this raises deeper questions about what authenticity truly means. Assuming authenticity means how we are in the present moment, then AI cannot work alongside identity construction in a way that maintains traditional notions of authenticity.

Attribution & AI Use

  • Tools used: DALL·E
  • AI prompts (summary): Generate a picture of me. I am an 18-year-old African American male. I am 5'8", and weight 185 pounds. I have an approximately 4-inch afro with a low taper. I have straight teeth, but one of my two front teeth is slightly larger than the other due to me chipping it when i was younger.
  • What AI generated: See above images
  • What you changed or decided: I then asked DALL-E to give me slightly larger muscles, to clean up my hair, and then to change the lighting to that which you would find in a studio. I also asked to be put into streetwear clothes, like baggy jeans.
