## Process Notes
I started off by thinking of what type of character I wanted my chatbot to encapsulate. After a second of thinking, I thought about wanting to personify a stereotypical action hero. One that on the surface is rough, but is caring deep down, only toughened because of this traumatic past. When it came to this, I was able to control these parameters when making my character AI, giving him this basis of a personality. The parameter that I was not able to control, however, was what the AI chatbot ultimately decided to generate. All I had the power to do was give the AI direction on what I wanted. What is generated in the end is completely up to the AI, and how closely it follows what I told it is up to it. When it came to the amount of outputs that I generated, it ended up totaling 17. This was not a predetermined amount of output that I wanted to reach, but rather was a natural stopping point that I arrived at after achieving what I wanted to with my character. The goal I was trying to achieve was seeing how fast it would take for the AI to break its initial character that I set in any capacity. Here, it only took around 13 lines for the AI to start to waiver and give a backstory that I specifically told it could never be announced outloud. It honestly took a lot longer than I thought it would, but I was confident that I would be able to.

## Reflection
Creativity with endless generation often ends up being mundane and repetitive. The human ability to create comes in the form of being able to make a story that has a beginning and an end, and is something that people want to follow due to being touched by the contents of what the narrative holds. There is a predetermined message trying to be conveyed, and seeing how it is unraveled is the best part of interacting with human creativity. So, what happens when humans try to get generative AI to make such stories is that the AI is unable to think of these facets of storytelling. It is unable to have an end goal in mind and must adapt to whatever is told. So what happens in the end, unfortunately, is that the AI is, in reality, limited by humans (to an extent). It must accommodate the ever-changing inputs of the human, outputting weaker and weaker narratives that end up being surface-level and unimpressive. Controlling the output of generative AI infinitely means controlling infinite and uninspiring possibilities.
