Week 10 – Games & Play

The Artifact

Process Notes

My game (made in Twine) is about a recent college graduate, who is now looking for a job. They are able to land an interview at a promising company, but when they get there, they discover that they will be interviewed by an A.I. program named A.H.A.. The rest of the game is the player making choices that determine whether or not they get the job, or if they even want the job in the first place. The choices that you make will determine the ending which you get, and there are 5 total endings that you can get. For example, in the game, you can choose to either go to sleep early or stay up late the night before your interview. The choice that you make there will determine what endings you will have access to later in the game, so it is important that you choose wisely to get the ending that you want. My game is supposed to act as a commentary on the new prominence that A.I. is starting to have on our everyday lives. The game is meant to make you ask how much autonomy and power we should give to A.I., and whether or not we should allow it the tools it needs to grow even further. Your personal values on A.I. are able to be translated into the game, and I believe for the most part that the ending that you achieve will reflect on what kind of person that you are and what you personally believe in when it comes to life and artificial intelligence.
