Week 3 – Selfie & Identity

Reflection Prompt

Reflect 3: What does "authenticity" mean when identity is co-constructed with AI?

Response

An "authentic" self cannot truly exist when co-constructing with AI. The pure, authentic self might exist in the raw image uploaded to AI models—representing us in our most physical form. However, the moment we use AI to manipulate this image, in any manner, we are no longer truly "authentic."

There exists an argument that AI can allow us to construct a form of ourselves that we see as true, but this raises deeper questions about what authenticity truly means. Assuming authenticity means how we are in the present moment, then AI cannot work alongside identity construction in a way that maintains traditional notions of authenticity.

Week 4 – Comic & Storytelling

Reflection Prompt

Reflect 4: Is AI a collaborator, tool, or plagiarist in storytelling?

Response

AI is a tool in the process of storytelling. When it comes to AI, it should be used as the means to an end to bring a creative idea forward. For example, take an artist who wants to create an artwork. The artist has many different media they can use to try and convey their message, from something as simple as pencils and crayons to a complex thing such as stone sculpting. Among these media, I believe AI can help to guide us to the correct one in which to use (to an extent).

When human input and thinking get the idea of a work to 90% completion, AI can help to bring that last 10% to reality and cement a direction to move in. We can now substitute the artist example for that of the storyteller. AI can help to guide thinking through giving a somewhat concrete example of what you want your end product to be. When AI is used in this way, I believe it is being used to its best ability.

Week 6 – Text & Distant Reading

Reflection Prompt

Reflect 6: What do we gain/lose when machines "read" literature for us?

Response

When machines read literature for us, we gain efficiency but lose competency. AI, as we have discussed previously, lacks a certain human "soul" or emotions, and while it can identify emotions in text, it simply does not have the capacity to truly understand them. So what does that mean when it interacts with literature, a very soulful and human medium? It makes for reflection that is devoid of any speculation or understanding.

Say that you want an AI to summarize a writing piece, for the sake of efficiency and time. The AI then pulls out a bulleted point summary of the reading, taking the subjective parts of it and putting it on the screen. You would be content with this theoretically, but let's say you then decide to actually read the passage to see how accurate it is. When you do, you see lots of small things that the AI missed. Non-essential or large ideas, but ones nonetheless that add to the overall meaning of the passage and interpretation of the reading.

The AI had one task: to summarize. But it was not told to interact with the text, to think about it critically, or to think about what it means. There is no prompt you can tell the AI that would make it do this, as it simply can't. This is what is lost with AI. Critical interaction that allows for the finding of hidden and subtle meaning.

Week 7 – Mapping AI Worlds

Reflection Prompt

Reflect 7: How does AI reshape global geographies of power, labor, and environment?

Response

We can see how AI reshapes global geographies of power, labor, and environment through its increasing importance in economic spheres worldwide. AI is a phenomenon that is not solely isolated to the United States, as more and more countries and their people are wanting and willing to incorporate this developing technology.

This increased demand for this service increases labor, as more people are being employed to build and operate such technologies, as well as to further the research and development of said technology. There is also a power component to AI as well, as these AI need local datacenters to operate to their maximum capacities. This allows these AI companies to go overseas and to build in countries that need these local data centers, furthering their own reach.

This, in turn, causes major environmental changes in areas where these centers are built, as it increases natural resources consumption exponentially. Overall, AI's increasing demand has reshaped and transformed the power, labor, and environmental dimensions of societies globally.

Week 8 – Networks of Knowledge & Power

Reflection Prompt

Reflect 8: Who is visible/invisible in AI networks? How does visualization reveal or obscure power?

Response

In AI networks, the final product is revealed, but the means stay hidden. Take the supply chain of AI resources as an example. If we want to track something like silicon, we start where it is mostly produced. We then arrive at China, and from here we can track where this resource goes, what is made, and what final product is ultimately constructed.

But one thing we do not see is right at the beginning, the actual production of silicon. Those who had to mine it, the actual local people responsible for this, are hidden from the public eye. This lack of visualization obscures these people's power, as it allows for large companies to benefit from their labor and keep them working these exploitative jobs.
