How I Use AI

My Approach to AI Integration

I approach AI as a supplementary tool to enhance my work, not as a replacement for human creativity and critical thinking. My philosophy is grounded in intentionality and careful curation: while AI can accelerate certain tasks, the majority of my work remains human-driven, with rigorous oversight over any generated content.

Tools I Use

I primarily use ChatGPT for most of my AI-assisted tasks, but I'm not limited to a single service. Depending on the project needs, I also explore other AI tools and services to find the best fit for specific applications.

Examples of My AI Prompts

My use cases span a wide range—from creative to analytical:

  • Image Generation: "Generate a picture of me. I am an 18-year-old African American male. I am 5'8, and weight 185 pounds. I have an approximately 4-inch afro with a low taper. I have straight teeth, but one of my two front teeth is slightly larger than the other due to me chipping it when i was younger."
  • Literary Analysis: "Summarize the poem, 'The Raven', by Edgar Allan Poe" and "Can you give me an in depth analysis"

These examples show how I leverage AI across different domains—whether generating visual content or analyzing text—always with the goal of exploring possibilities rather than accepting outputs uncritically.

My Curation Process

What I keep and what I discard depends entirely on my own personal assessment of quality. I don't accept AI-generated content at face value. Instead, I:

  • Evaluate outputs critically against my own standards and requirements
  • Verify accuracy and relevance before incorporating generated content
  • Trust my judgment to determine whether something adds value to my work

This careful approach ensures that only content that meets my standards makes it into my final projects.

My AI Use Policy

I believe that 95% of my work should be human-driven, with 5% potentially leveraging AI—and even that 5% requires rigorous monitoring and review.

This means:

  • The majority of ideation, creation, and decision-making is my responsibility
  • AI assists with specific, well-defined tasks where it genuinely adds value
  • Every piece of generated content is reviewed and refined to ensure quality and authenticity
  • I maintain creative and intellectual ownership of my work, even when using AI as a tool

The goal is not to minimize human effort, but to use AI strategically while preserving the integrity and authenticity of my work.

Limits, Bias, and Ethical Considerations

The Limits of AI

Through this course, I've learned that AI has significant limitations. When generating images of specific cultural phenomena, for example, AI struggles to authentically represent lived experiences and cultural nuance. An AI cannot capture the true essence of traditions, customs, or cultural identity because it hasn't experienced these things firsthand. This is a critical limitation that means AI-generated cultural content should be approached with extreme caution or avoided altogether when authenticity and respect are paramount.

Bias in AI Systems

AI systems are trained on existing data, which often contains historical biases and inequities. This means AI can inadvertently perpetuate stereotypes, misrepresent marginalized communities, or produce outputs that reflect the biases present in their training data. I'm particularly conscious of this when using image generation tools—the outputs may not fairly represent the diversity and complexity of human experience. Recognizing this bias is essential to using AI responsibly.

Ethical Considerations

The ethical use of AI requires careful thought:

  • Authenticity: AI should not be used to generate content that claims to represent authentic human experiences or cultural knowledge it doesn't possess
  • Transparency: I openly acknowledge when and how I use AI in my work, allowing others to understand the role of automation in my creative process
  • Human Agency: AI should enhance human capability, not replace human judgment, creativity, or responsibility
  • Data and Training: I consider where AI systems source their training data and the potential harms embedded in those datasets

These considerations inform every decision I make about whether and how to integrate AI into my work.
