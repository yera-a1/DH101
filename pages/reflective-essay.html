What Does It Mean to Be Human in the Age of AI?

To be human in the age of AI means being in charge of your own tools and means. Going into this course, my own thoughts towards AI were dismissive at best. I thought of its usage as gimmicky, unproductive, and overall uninspired when compared to the human process of creation. Sitting in on the first class of the semester, I believed that I would continue this way of thinking throughout the course of the year. But over time, from make to make, week to week, my viewpoints began to change. As I was forced more and more to defend against any challenges to my view on AI and engage in civil discussion of issues surrounding this developing technology, I realized that something in me had changed. Throughout the course of this class, I have come to understand the validity of artificial intelligence as a tool for creation. I no longer saw it as only a soulless and unable creation, but as a way to enhance us as humans.

For instance, during our final make (Futures of AI & Humanity), we were tasked with designing a theoretical future for AI. I had an idea that I wanted to execute with an AI eviction notice, but did not have the artistic means to create such a thing myself. But understanding the course I was in, I turned to the usage of AI to bridge the gap between my inability to make and my creative ideas. I was then able to generate an image to go along with my human-made text, and I was able to complete the assignment.

This point can be found and illustrated through the multitude of different readings we have done throughout the class. Beginning with "On the Dangers of Stochastic Parrots: Can Language Models Be Too Big?", we can see how even the creation of AI requires a level of humanity to ensure the utmost quality. The core of the reading revolves around AI language models and how they can be detrimental to both the environment and the minorities of languages they work to represent. A consideration made at the end of the article is that there needs to be "a mindset of careful planning…before starting to build either datasets or systems trained on datasets." What is shown here is that even with the creation of AI, and not even its usage, there needs to be a certain amount of human consideration and input to ensure the best possible outcome.

The next reading that I would like to consider is Critical Making in the Age of AI, specifically the chapter regarding the selfie. Here we can see how AI itself is only a tool that can not truly understand human topics on its own. We see in this article how the "ubiquitous" boom of AI picture-generating tools has caused fear of creating a world of "defaults and stereotypes", caused by "simple queries" giving the most "reductive output". When used without imagination, AI runs the risk of stunting human creativity, and it is our responsibility as people to ensure we generate responsibly and with purpose.

The final reading I want to reference from class is Unflatening. I feel as if this demonstrates the most prevailing danger of AI to humanity, this being our potential loss of autonomy and sense of self. Through the visuals and text of this reading, we see a society where everyone has conformed to one way of thinking, where the concept of critical thinking has been done away with, a "single chorus." When we overuse AI, giving up the reins and letting it think for us, we lose what makes us special. We lose our ability to create, to express ourselves, to share our own experiences with the world, and in this place, we become a homogenous and conformed world. Remaining in control of AI means remaining in control of our humanity, and with it, who we are.

When approaching human interaction with artificial intelligence, the overall takeaway should be that AI exists to help us. It allows us to do things that we would otherwise not be able to do, and to engage with problems in different ways. The most important thing to remember when using AI is to remain in control. As I have said time and time again, AI is a tool. It is not supposed to be the main contributor, the main thinker; its sole purpose is to serve a supporting role. To be human is to realize this, and to go out and create with AI, not letting AI create you.
